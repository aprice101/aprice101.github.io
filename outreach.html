Amy Price PhD - Outreach activities

It is plain if you pay attention to the news and the world around you that not everyone has an equal chance to do well in life. Inequality is intersectional; to fight inequality we must address all sources of discrimination, from racism, sexism, homophobia, and ableism to economic policies that keep the poor impoverished.

We can all do our part to help fix this- whether that's voting, having conversations with people about how to stop inequality and the injustices associated with inequality or donating to causes fighting inequality. Let's work together towards an equitable future for all.

Outreach activities

Bay Area Scientists Inspiring Students BASIS

I volunteer as part of a team providing free science lessons in elementary school classrooms around the Bay Area. The aim of this program is to provide exciting and hands on science activities to young people. We aim to inspire students to envision themselves as future scientists.

Expanding Your Horizons Conference EYH

Expanding Your Horizons conferences are designed to introduce 5th-8th grade girls to careers in science, technology, engineering and mathematics. From fun hands-on workshops, to discussions about careers in STEM and panels where the students can ask scientists anything, this conference encourages girls to take up careers in STEM. I took part last year as a workshop volunteer, and hope to do so again this year.

Berkeley Science Review BSR

The Berkeley Science Review is a magazine run by grad students to help make some of the discoveries and work carried out by scientists at UC Berkeley and Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory accessible to a wide audience. I have written and edited for the BSR over the past year and a half; keep an eye out for our upcoming issue in Nov' 22!
