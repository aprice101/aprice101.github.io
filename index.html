Inorganic Synthetic Chemistry - From the main group to the actinides

About me

I am currently an associate lecturer in inorganic chemistry at the University of St Andrews. Previously I was a postdoc conducting research into the properties of the f-block elements at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory and University of California Berkeley in Prof. P. Arnold's group. I obtained my PhD in 2018 after working with Dr. Michael Cowley at the University of Edinburgh on reactive phosphorus and boron containing molecules. I spent a year in industry with Johnson Matthey developing improved plant processes during my MChem degree.

Research Interests

I am currently an education focused lecturer. Previously I worked on multiple projects in the P. Arnold and M. Cowley research groups; these ranged in focus from rare earth separations to fundamental heavy element chemistry to the chemistry of low-coordinate main group molecules. One aim that unites my research projects is to improve our understanding of the basic properties of some of the elements. This understanding is vital so that we can better separate our waste streams, can predict the properties of functional materials and for understanding the chemistry at the very limits of the periodic table.
